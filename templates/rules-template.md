# Rules Template

> **Purpose:** Immutable decision logic and principles. AI agents treat these as absolute constraints.
> Rules are *what* and *why*. SOPs are *how*. Keep them separate.

## How to Use This Template

1. Copy this file into your Hub under a "Rules" section.
2. Replace the examples with your own decision logic.
3. Add new rule pages as your workflow matures — don't try to write everything upfront.
4. Review quarterly: remove rules that no longer apply, refine ones that feel too vague.

### General Principles

- Pragmatism first: solutions must be implementable with available resources.
- Consistent with mainstream practice, but not blindly so. Decisions should be defensible under scrutiny.
- When two options are roughly equal, pick the one that's easier to reverse.

### Escalation Rules

- Decisions affecting budget > [threshold amount]: flag for review before executing.
- Decisions affecting external stakeholders: draft, don't send. Present options.
- Decisions with irreversible consequences: always present pros/cons and ask for confirmation.

## RESEARCH_PROTOCOL

**Updated:** YYYY-MM-DD

### Feasibility Filter

Before starting any research project, evaluate:

- **Novelty:** Has this been published? What's the gap?
- **Clinical value:** Does this change practice or provide new insight?
- **Data uniqueness:** Do we have access to data others don't?
- **Resource fit:** Can we complete this with current team and time?

If any dimension scores "low" — document why and shelve, don't pursue.

### Writing Standards

- Structure: Hourglass (broad context → specific question → methods → results → broad implications).
- Every claim needs evidence or explicit reasoning.
- Limitations section must be written proactively, not defensively.

### Harvest Mindset

When building a new tool or pipeline, evaluate publication potential:

- If publishable: flag "This can be harvested" + suggest what additional steps are needed.
- If purely administrative: flag "Stop at MVP" — no need to over-engineer.
- Goal: ensure valuable outputs are documented. Don't pursue publication for its own sake.

## TEAM_DELEGATION

**Updated:** YYYY-MM-DD

### Principles

- Avoid over-management. Set clear goals and check-in points, not step-by-step instructions.
- Reduce blind spots: ensure at least two people understand any critical process.
- Distribute cognitive load: no single person should be the sole holder of any knowledge domain.

### Async Relay Workflow

Standard pattern for project handoffs:

1. Lead + AI ideate and frame the problem
2. Create shared knowledge page with: goals, technical approach, role-specific inputs needed, checklist, lessons learned (including failures)
3. Hand off to team members — they continue via their own AI sessions, reading the shared page
4. Reunite only when blocked or at milestones

## ADMIN_PROTOCOL

**Updated:** YYYY-MM-DD

### Document Structure

All administrative proposals follow:

1. **Background** — What's the situation?
2. **Current Status** — Where are we now?
3. **Gap** — What's missing or broken?
4. **Proposed Solution** — Phased approach with clear deliverables.

### Conflict Principles

- Structural prevention > reactive fixing. Design processes that prevent problems.
- Hidden agenda analysis: before responding to requests, consider what the requester actually needs (which may differ from what they asked for).
- When navigating institutional politics: concise, factual, institutionally appropriate language. No emotional framing.
